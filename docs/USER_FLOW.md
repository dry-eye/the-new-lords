# New Lords — User Flow

Short, concrete play-through flows through the actual mechanics — not abstract wants. Each step names the real system it exercises.

## Flow 1: The Entrepreneur

1. Start screen → pick the entrepreneur starting kit: a lone character, no org, a small stake of personal money.
2. Found a business-kind org, staffed via the worker pool as an employee.
3. Sell surplus output at nearby settlements (the buy/sell merchant action), reinvest profit into a second enterprise.
4. Cross the first perk milestone → take a business-kind perk (e.g. Guild Charter): more output, but locked out of ever nationalizing.
5. Grow wealthy enough to open an investment slot into the local political org — buy policy influence without conquest.
6. Optional endpoint: once wealth/influence is high enough, `BecomePolitical` — the business itself becomes the seed of a new political org.

## Flow 2: The Bandit / Mercenary

Both branches start the same way: a squad-kind org with no settlement and no political standing. The branch point is the first real decision — raid, or hire out.

**Branch A — conquest.**
1. Raid weakly-defended settlements, hold the loot as squad property.
2. Capture a settlement outright.
3. Automatic kind change: the bandit squad-org converts into an independent political org, leadership type **Warlord** — plays like a monarch from here (own territory, own tax policy, own perk track). Warlord is conquest-only — it never appears from the normal random-leader pool.
4. Continue growing as any political org would: more territory, perks, diplomacy with peer sides.

**Branch B — infiltration.**
1. Instead of raiding, take a paid control slot inside an existing state's org — hired service, salary from their treasury.
2. Build loyalty and reputation while garrisoning or fighting for them.
3. Spend earned money to open a covert-influence slot inside the same state — a hidden second relationship alongside the visible hire.
4. Use that covert influence, plus accumulated internal loyalty, to tilt an internal-factions vote your way or back a rival claimant during a succession crisis.
5. Endpoint: your squad-org ends up effectively running the state from the inside — no war declared, no settlement taken by force.
